High Fidelity Emotional Speech Synthesis with Robust Style Conditioning and Attention Alignment

Byoung Jin Choi Byung Chan Son Sung Jun Cheon Minchan Kim Nam Soo Kim
[Code]

Abstract

In this letter, we present the state-of-the-art emotional end-to-end (e2e) speech synthesis with robust emotion conditioning and attention alignment. The previous global style token (GST) based emotional speech synthesis approaches confirmed the feasibility of emotion conditioning in e2e speech synthesis. However, these approaches eliminate a large amount of valuable non-textual information by compressing the entire reference speech into a single fixed-dimensional vector, causing insufficient emotion conditioning. Moreover, the ongoing issue of unstable alignment in autoregressive e2e models is further amplified with an emotional speech synthesis scenario. To mitigate these shortcomings, we propose two major improvements to Transformer-based text-to-speech system (Transformer-TTS) using a reference speech. Firstly, in addition to global style information, local style information is introduced to condition the model via a separate style attention. Secondly, a speech-aligned alignment representation utilizing predicted duration information and an encoder-decoder attention masking are introduced to form robust alignment. The experiments demonstrate that the proposed model outperforms Tacotron2 with GST (Tacotron2-GST) in terms of speech quality, style similarity to the reference speech, and robust attention alignment.
